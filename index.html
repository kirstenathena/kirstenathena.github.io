Health Insurance Shopping Guide

Choosing the right health insurance for you and your family is an important decision. We understand, and we want you to feel confident in your choice. Use this guide to help you find the insurance plan that's right for you. Let's get started!

keyboard_arrow_down

What type of coverage do you qualify for?

Know which ConnectorCare plans are available for you

First, you'll need to know which plans are available to you, based on where you live. You can find the ConnectorCare plans available in your area in your renewal letter (if you are already a member), or by signing into your account and shopping for plans from the "My Eligibility" page.

Compare your monthly premium costs

Your ConnectorCare plan choices have different monthly premium costs, depending on the insurance company offering the plan. Covered benefits and out-of-pocket costs are the same for all plans.

Check the providers and prescription drugs covered by ConnectorCare plans

It's very important to check to see if providers you want to use for care (such as doctors, hospitals, or health centers) and any prescription drugs you need are covered by a plan before you enroll. To find out which ConnectorCare plans cover your providers and prescription drugs, use our online plan comparison tool.

Know your premium and make a payment

Once you choose a plan online or by calling Customer Service to enroll by phone, you'll need to pay your first premium (if you have one) to complete enrollment. Payment is always due by the 23rd of the month before your coverage begins.

Know which Health Connector plans are available for you

First, you’ll need to know which plans are available to you. If you applied online, you’ll be able to see your plan choices after you complete your application and click the Find a Plan button. If you applied by paper application or by phone, you can search for plans available in your area by going to the account landing page and clicking on Browse Plans halfway down the homepage. You can also call Customer Service.

Compare costs

The total cost of your coverage will include your monthly premium payments and your out-of-pocket costs. In general, the lower your monthly premium, the higher your out-of-pocket costs will be when you get covered services.

Our plans are organized by metallic tiers (levels) to make plan premiums and out-of-pocket costs easier to compare.

Check the providers and prescription drugs covered by plans

Once you’ve narrowed your plan choices down, check to see if providers you want to use for care (such as doctors, hospitals, or health centers) and any prescription drugs you need are covered by a plan before you enroll. Find out which plans cover your providers and prescription drugs using our online tool.

Choose your new plan and enroll!

After you’ve learned more about a plan’s costs and checked to see if the providers you want are in its network, you will need to complete your enrollment. You can enroll online at MAhealthconnector.org, or call Customer Service to enroll by phone. After you choose a plan, you’ll need to pay your first premium to complete enrollment. Payment is always due by the 23rd of the month before your coverage begins.
